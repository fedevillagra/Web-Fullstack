What's the best type of page for you?

Online store

Who use it? Companies

Purpose: online sale of physical products or services

Online store

Products can be sold well through landing pages, but an online store is needed for several products.


The products and their characteristics are presented in online stores without any problem. A clear navigation menu helps users find a suitable offer quickly. Content in the form of guides or informative articles can be integrated to attract Google users to your online store, but the main focus of attention remains on the product catalog.


Large electronic stores like Zalando or Amazon follow a similar structure:


The Amazon website is an example of a typical online store where the products are the center of attention (source: https://www.amazon.com/)


The technical implementation of an online store is particularly difficult because it includes the entire ordering and payment process. With tools like the IONOS web builder, small shops can be created. For larger projects, you can use a specialized agency.

Blog/Magazine

Who use it? Bloggers, companies, journalists, influencers

Purpose: regular editorial information or entertainment

Blog/Magazine

A blog or magazine website focuses on editorial content. Do you want to regularly report on bitcoins? Are you planning to create a local online newspaper? While blogs sometimes welcome readers with a static home page, online magazine home pages often resemble classic print magazines. They directly display the latest content.


Some blogs and online magazines also offer products and services related to your topic, but this is rather secondary. Current and regular content plays the leading role and this is also reflected in the design of the website.


This can be seen on the Smashing Magazine website:


The Smashing Magazine website focuses on current content, which is consistently reflected in structure, layout, and writing (source: https://www.smashingmagazine.com/)


However, maintaining a blog is a time-consuming process. Think if you can maintain your blog or online magazine on your own or if it would be better to find a team of writers. If you want your blog to become a reliable source of information and a magnet for readers, there should be no time gaps between posting articles.

Corporate Website

Who use it? Companies, agencies and freelancers

Purpose: presentation of the company itself with the possibility of establishing contact

Corporate Website

The classic corporate website corresponds to what is associated with a web page in general. It is a collection of several individual pages with the main purpose of providing information.


The company introduces its services, its team and offers networking opportunities. A corporate website is the company's digital business card. However, websites can also contain dynamic elements, for example a news area or a blog, the content of which is regularly updated. Larger websites often integrate other types of web pages, such as landing pages or microsites, so that businesses can pursue different goals with a single online presence.


To make your own brand or website known to the target audience, companies should take additional measures like search engine marketing.


An example of a classic corporate website is the KPMG website:


The website of the KPMG consultancy is a classic corporate website, with the focus on information (source: https://home.kpmg/xx/en/home.html)


If you want to create a corporate website yourself and have little or no programming skills, you can use website builders like the MyWebsite creation service. This makes it easy to get professional results. You can also hire an agency or, if you want to save your budget, you can use IONOS's MyWebsite design service to have experts implement a website according to your specifications.

Website app

Who use it? Tech companies, SaaS companies

Purpose: presentation of an online application

Website app

Web applications are not websites advertising a product: they are the product itself. Whether it's project management, financial management, or travel expense accounting, more and more software doesn't require a download, but can be used in the browser as a web application.


It is true that even a web application needs a few pages of a classic corporate website to inform about features, benefits and prices, but unlike an online business card, the user can use the tool after registering.


Trello is one of the most popular project management tools. The app is designed as a web app. Users only need Internet access and can access the application from anywhere using a browser. Especially for remote teams, web applications are a practical solution.


After signing up, users can use the Trello web app directly from their browser (source: https://trello.com/)

Landing page

Who use it? Companies, freelancers

Purposes : generation of potential leads or completion of the purchase

Landing page

The so-called landing pages are onepages that inform the visitor about a specific product, with the aim of motivating him to take a certain action. The page has a clear advertising character. Subpages are not used, but they resemble a detailed sales newsletter, which can be enriched with videos and graphics.


In the best case scenario, the user consumes the content and is willing to pay for a physical or digital product or service or to enter their email address.


If you're looking for inspiration to create a landing page, you'll find it on the American Association for Writers (AWAI) website. The copywriters association advertises its courses using classic landing pages:


The American Association for Writers uses landing pages to market their online courses as the “Accelerated Program for Six-Figure Copywriting” (source: https://www.awai.com/copywriting/p/)


Most of these pages are designed as part of online marketing campaigns: users click on an ad on Google or a social network and are redirected to the page.

Portfolio

Who use it? Artists and creatives

Purpose: presentation of mainly visual work samples

Portfolio

Portfolio web pages are optimized to present visual work in an attractive way. This type of website is ideal for designers, photographers or artists.


Website themes for portfolios are characterized by a minimalist design, which already on the page puts the focus on graphic elements and samples of the artist's work. Unlike corporate websites, the amount of text is usually small.


A portfolio website is very easy to create for non-technical people. CMS like WordPress offer a huge selection of themes, which you can easily adapt and fill with your content. The only technical hurdle is hosting. Choose a provider that specializes in WordPress hosting and supports a one-click WordPress installation. IONOS offers affordable prices for WordPress Hosting plans.


How can a website be a portfolio? Portrait photographer Martin Schoeller's website is a fairly typical example of this type of website:


Creative people's websites are often designed as a portfolio (source: https://martinschoeller.com/)


If you want to create a portfolio website of your own, you need to be careful and submit only the best work samples. It's better to undersubmit and include your best work than to overload the site with too many mediocre references.
